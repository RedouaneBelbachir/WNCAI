About the Conference

The First International Conference on Wireless Terrestrial and Non-Terrestrial Communication and AI is a premier event bringing together researchers, industry experts, and innovators from around the world. This year's conference addresses the role of AI in Terrestrial and satellite communication and their contribution to the economy of the future where flying cars, drones, vehicles, IoT and people are always connected to the network. The Conference offers a forum for world-renowned scientists, engineers, and practitioners to present their latest research results, ideas, developments, and applications of NTN for 5G and 6G.

  • Date: December 20-22, 2025
  • Location: ENPO, Oran
  • Theme: "Shaping the Future with Technology"

Conference Tracks

The areas of interest include, but are not limited to:

Track 1: 5G, 6G, and beyond

  • Physical layers technologies and approaches.
  • Advanced RF beam forming.
  • PHY Layer Security and Secure communication solutions.
  • MAC and Energy-efficient medium access strategies.
  • Advanced physical layers design and optimization.
  • Vehicular networks in 5G/6G/B6G.
  • V2X (Vehicle-to-Everything) communications and autonomous driving.
  • Channel coding/autoencoder techniques.
  • Error correction mechanisms.
  • Software Defined Radio (SDR) and Cognitive Radio.

Track 2: Artificial Intelligence and Machine Learning Fundamentals

  • Artificial Intelligent Theory and Systems
  • Economic Paradigms and Game Theory
  • AI for Computer vision and visual recognition
  • Blockchain in Machine Learning
  • Distributed Knowledge Processing
  • Security Networks and Systems based-on AI/ML techniques
  • AI/ML applications in Computers and Communications
  • Machine learning, data mining for cloud computing
  • Emerging AI/ML for Big Data, data analytics and distributed architectures.
  • Distributed Knowledge and Processing based-on AI
  • AI for Robotics Technologies and Human-Robot Interactions
  • Data mining and predictive analytics.

Track 3: Communication Systems, IoT and space engineering

  • AI contribution for antenna conceptions.
  • Fog and Mobile Edge Computing in unmanned aerial vehicle communications and applications.
  • IoT: From Sensors to Vertical Applications
  • IoT security for industry 4.0, supply chain and blockchain.
  • Satellite IoT and Environmental monitoring and disaster response.
  • Communication Networks and space missions driving.
  • Nano Communications and Networking.
  • Testbeds, Experimentation and Datasets for Communications and Networking
  • Data twins for precision agriculture, smart city and industry 4.0 applications.
  • AI-Powered Services for Drone Design and Deployment.
  • AI Applications in Electromagnetic Compatibility (EMC).
  • Baseband Signal Processing, Adaptive Filtering Using AI.
  • ML services for RF systems design.
  • Satellite sub-systems development and deployment.
  • High-Capacity Transmission Techniques Enhanced by AI.

Track 4: Student papers and activities

  • Student Papers: Graduate students are invited to submit papers on any of the above tracks with a focus on innovative ideas or early-stage research.
  • Poster Sessions: Students will have the opportunity to present their work in a visual format, allowing them to engage in open discussions with visiting researchers and attendees from around the world. An excellent opportunity to share your work, receive feedback from experts, and contribute to the advancement of your field of interest.
  • Demo Sessions: Students will be able to showcase their prototypes, solutions, or simulations, demonstrating practical applications of their theoretical work. This hands-on presentation format highlights innovation and technical skills, making it an ideal platform for sharing tangible results.
  • Workshops/Tutorials: Hands-on sessions will be organized for students as well as interested academics and industry professionals to learn about emerging technologies such as IoT, Big Data and the above tracks. This session provides practical knowledge, tools, and methodologies that can be directly applied to ongoing research and projects.
